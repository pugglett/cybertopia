Direct and Indirect Objects

Direct objects: a noun or pronoun that directly receives the action of the verb.

EX: Dante threw the ball.

Ball is the direct object. Dante took the BALL and threw it.

Challenges with direct objects include:

  • A verb may have two direct objects and when asking a question, the direct object may be the interrogative pronoun what, which, or whom.
  • It cannot be where, how, or when. These are adverbs. Sometimes it helps to keep all words in the sentence but change it into a declarative sentence.

EX: Bob hit the trash can and the mail box on his way out of the driveway.

Bob hit both the trash can and the mail box thus two direct objects.

EX: What did you buy?

You bought what?

Indirect objects: is a noun that receives the action the verb, but the action is indirect because it affects the direct object first.

Not all sentences that have a direct object also have an indirect object, but all sentences with an indirect object must have a direct object.

Indirect objects ALWAYS come before the direct object.

EX: I threw Jonas the ball.

Ball is the direct object. Jonas is the indirect object because he received the ball. The ball I threw was directed to him.
